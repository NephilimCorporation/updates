What does an ID such as FU-APP-2023-001 mean?

These IDs are called Update Ciphers. They are used for the systematic classification and organization of software updates. Consider "FU-APP-2023-001," which offers insights beyond its characters. "FU" signifies a "Feature Update," indicating substantial changes and new functionalities. "APP" likely represents a specific software application or project, adding clarity. "2023" acts as a timestamp, indicating the year of release, while "001" conveys that it's the first feature update within that year. Update Ciphers provide a structured framework for distinguishing between different types of updates, whether it's "FUs" (Feature Updates) introducing new features or "HFs" (Hotfixes) resolving specific issues. These ciphers help streamline update management, offering developers and users a concise yet informative way to understand the nature, source, timeline, and sequence of each update, enhancing the efficiency and organization of the software update process.
